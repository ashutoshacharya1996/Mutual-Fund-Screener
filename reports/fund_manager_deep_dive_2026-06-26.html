// WHY "ALPHA" IS OFTEN NOT ALPHA

Professional fund analysts decompose returns using the Carhart 4-Factor Model:

Return = Risk-Free Rate + β(Market) + β(SMB: Size) + β(HML: Value) + β(MOM: Momentum) + True Alpha

  • A small-cap fund beating the Nifty 50 is not generating alpha — it's earning a size premium (SMB) that any small-cap index fund captures.
  • A fund beating its benchmark during a quality-factor bull run (2016–2021) may simply have quality factor loading, not manager skill.
  • Quant's high-turnover strategy likely has significant momentum factor (MOM) loading — meaning some of its "alpha" could be replicated by a momentum index fund.
  • The residual after all four factors is genuine alpha. For most Indian funds, this residual is near zero or negative after fees.

// VLRT FRAMEWORK — HOW IT ACTUALLY WORKS

V — Valuation: Goes beyond conventional PE/PB. Proprietary cross-asset valuation comparisons. Claim: standard metrics "lead to inaccurate conclusions." Actual indicators not disclosed.

L — Liquidity: The most differentiated element. Tracks global and domestic money flows using "alternate data sources" — central bank balance sheets, FII flow patterns, credit creation, derivatives-implied liquidity. Measures whether global liquidity is expanding or contracting. Key insight: liquidity dries up at market tops and bottoms.

R — Risk Appetite: Options market signals (put/call ratios, IV term structure), cash market breadth, institutional positioning. Measures fear vs. greed cycles.

T — Timing: Cycle analytics using historical data going back "centuries." Identifies medium-term turning points by combining V, L, R signals.

Dynamic weighting (critical): VLRT weights change by market regime. COVID example — March 2020: L + R = 90% weight, V = 10%. Rationale: valuation didn't matter during a liquidity crisis; what mattered was whether central banks would step in (they would).

Quantamental overlay: VLRT + factor scoring (Growth + Value + Quality factors) + human judgment. The Quantamental fund is the hybrid product.

Turnover consequence: Style-agnostic, high-conviction repositioning = ~500% portfolio turnover. The portfolio is fully replaced ~5 times per year. This generates significant STCG within the fund, which is a structural tax drag on NAV for Indian fund investors.

§5   Professional Fund Evaluation Frameworks
// WHAT CFA-LEVEL ANALYSTS ACTUALLY USE
FRAMEWORK 1 — INFORMATION RATIO (IR): THE MOST IMPORTANT METRIC

Formula: IR = Active Return ÷ Tracking Error  =  (Fund Return – Benchmark Return) ÷ Std Dev of Active Return

Why it beats raw alpha: A fund generating +3% alpha every single year has a high IR. A fund generating +8% one year and –4% the next has a low IR — despite the same average. IR rewards consistency of outperformance, not just magnitude. SEBI now mandates IR disclosure for all mutual fund schemes.

Benchmarks: IR > 0.5 = meaningful. IR > 0.75 = strong. IR > 1.0 = exceptional (rare in any market).

Gap: Aggregated IR data for your specific funds is not freely accessible — requires Primeinvestor or Morningstar India premium subscription. This is the single most important data point you should get.

FRAMEWORK 2 — CARHART 4-FACTOR MODEL: SEPARATING SKILL FROM BETA

Model: Return = Rf + β·Market + β·SMB (size) + β·HML (value) + β·MOM (momentum) + Alpha

What it reveals for your funds:

• Bandhan SC's 29% 3Y return: How much is small-cap beta (SMB)? In 2021–24, Nifty Smallcap 250 itself returned strongly. Strip out SMB factor and the genuine alpha may be much smaller than raw 29% suggests.

• Quant ELSS's strong returns: High-turnover, style-agnostic fund likely has large MOM factor loading. If so, a momentum index fund could replicate part of the return at lower cost and with fewer governance risks.

• Axis Midcap's 2016–2021 run: Likely had Quality factor loading (not in Carhart but in Fama-French 5-factor). The quality factor ran hot from 2016–2021, then gave back gains. How much of the 0.87% net alpha is genuine stock-picking vs. quality factor drift?

Academic finding: Indian study (arXiv 2024) — applying Carhart with style drift adjustment shows residual alpha of only 0.02%/month for style-drifting Indian funds. Most "alpha" is unexplained factor exposure.

FRAMEWORK 3 — ROLLING RETURN HIT RATE

Method: Calculate fund return for every possible 3-year window from inception. What % beat the benchmark?

Why point-to-point returns mislead: A fund starting in 2019 and showing 5-year returns in 2024 captures the post-COVID bull run. Rolling returns eliminate start-date dependency.

What to look for: 70%+ of 3-year rolling windows beating benchmark = strong. Mirae Asset L&M: "good but not always consistent" rolling outperformance per Primeinvestor. Axis Midcap: 2021–22 windows pulled down the rolling hit rate substantially.

How to check: Value Research Online → Fund → "Rolling Returns" tab. Free for most data. This is the single most accessible tool available to you.

FRAMEWORK 4 — ACTIVE SHARE (CLOSET INDEXING DETECTION)

Definition: % of portfolio holdings that differ from benchmark. 0% = pure index fund; 100% = completely different from benchmark.

Thresholds: Above 60% = genuinely active. 20–60% = closet indexing (charging active fees for near-index returns). Below 20% = effectively passive.

Estimated active share for your funds: Axis Midcap = high (70–90%), Mirae L&M = moderate-high, Bandhan SC = high (small-cap is inherently high active share), Quant = very high (style-agnostic, high turnover). None of your funds are likely closet indexers.

Note: Indian AMCs don't mandatorily disclose active share. Above are inferences from portfolio structure. SEBI is likely to mandate this disclosure in future.

FRAMEWORK 5 — SEBI STRESS TEST (INDIA-SPECIFIC, MANDATORY)

What it measures: Days required to liquidate 25% and 50% of portfolio at average daily trading volumes. Mandated monthly from March 2024.

Why it matters more than 1-year returns for small-cap evaluation: During a market panic, a fund needing 50 days to liquidate 50% faces forced selling at progressively worse prices — creating NAV spiral. This is an existential risk for investors who need to redeem during a crisis.
